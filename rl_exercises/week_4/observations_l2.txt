Level 2 observations: RLiable vs Plain Averages

Ran the DQN with the best L1 configuration (width=128, depth=3, buffer=20000, batch=64) across 10 different random seeds for 5000 frames each.

Summary statistics:
- IQM (Interquartile Mean): 76.00 (95% CI: 58.38 - 92.00)
- Mean: 74.90 (95% CI: 61.32 - 88.70)
- Median: 73.20 (95% CI: 55.00 - 94.30)

Key differences: RLiable vs Plain Averages
1. IQM is slightly higher than mean, which suggests the middle 50% of runs performed a bit better than the full average. That makes IQM the better summary of typical performance.
2. Mean and median are close, which suggests the distribution is not strongly skewed, but the gap to IQM still shows why a robust metric is useful.
3. Using bootstrap CIs, the intervals are still fairly wide and sometimes asymmetric. This reflects substantial run-to-run variability even after averaging over 10 seeds.

Confidence in results:
- The bootstrap CIs are narrower than with fewer seeds, but they still leave meaningful uncertainty.
- IQM remains the preferred robust metric: it downweights extremes and better represents the typical mid-range performance across seeds.
- The individual seed trajectories show substantial differences, so reporting only a single run or plain mean would still be misleading.
