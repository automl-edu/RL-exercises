1. EXPERIMENT SETUP
-------------------
The experiment evaluates the impact of 3 hyperparameters on the agent's performance:
- Network Architecture: Small [32, 32], Medium [64, 64], Large [128, 128, 128].
- Batch Size: 32, 64, 128.
- Replay Buffer Size: 5000, 10000, 20000.

Total configurations tested: 9.
Training duration: 20,000 frames per configuration.

2. PARAMETER ANALYSIS
---------------------

A. Impact of Network Architecture:
- Observation: 
    + The "small" architecture [32, 32] achieved the highest and most stable rewards (~210). 
    + The "large" architecture [128, 128, 128] performed the worst (~180).
- Insight: 
    + For simple environments, deeper/wider networks increase optimization complexity and may lead to overfitting. 
    + The model capacity must be balanced with the task's complexity.

B. Impact of Batch Size:
- Observation: 
    + A Batch Size of 64 proved to be the sweet spot, reaching peak rewards (~250). 
    + Batch Size 32 showed steady but slower learning. 
    + Batch Size 128 exhibited high instability and performance drops after an initial quick start.
- Insight: 
    + A batch size that is too large reduces the frequency of gradient updates per frame, making the agent less responsive to the rapid changes occurring during the exploration phase.

C. Impact of Replay Buffer Size:
- Observation: 
    + A larger buffer (20,000) showed a slight trend toward better performance compared to smaller buffers (5,000 and 10,000). 
    + However, the performance gap was less significant than those caused by changes in batch size or network depth.
- Insight: 
    + While a larger buffer helps in decorrelating transitions and stabilizing training, the relatively small state space means even a moderate buffer size can sufficiently cover the necessary experience.

3. KEY CONCLUSIONS
------------------
- Optimal Configuration: Batch Size 64, Architecture [32, 32], and Buffer Size 20,000.
- Critical Trade-off: The balance between convergence speed and training stability is highly sensitive to Batch Size.
- Efficiency: For simple RL control tasks, shallow networks paired with medium-sized batches offer the most efficient training path.
