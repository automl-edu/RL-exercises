Level 1 observations

I ran three CartPole DQN ablations for 5 seeds each and 2500 frames per seed.

Configuration results (final mean reward / peak):
- small (width=32, depth=1, buffer=2000, batch=32): 16.85 / 23.5
- baseline (width=64, depth=2, buffer=10000, batch=32): 13.53 / 23.5
- large (width=128, depth=3, buffer=20000, batch=64): 18.13 / 23.5

All three models reached about the same highest reward (~23.5) near the beginning of training. This shows that even smaller networks can learn the basic CartPole task quickly.
The large model performed the best overall by the end of training, with a final mean reward of 18.13. Its bigger network, larger replay buffer, and bigger batch size may have helped it learn more stable behavior over time.
The baseline model ended with the lowest final reward (13.53). One possible reason is that its replay buffer was not large enough to keep training stable later in learning.
The small model still achieved a fairly good final reward (16.85), close to the large model. This suggests that simply increasing network width may not improve performance much for CartPole.
The results are somewhat unstable, so longer training and testing with multiple random seeds would give more reliable conclusions.
