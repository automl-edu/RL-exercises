Q1: How does the length of the trajectories affect the training?
-------------------
Answer:

The length of the trajectories significantly impacts the training in primarily three ways: 

1. High Variance: Longer trajectories accumulate more random state transitions and actions. This causes the calculated return (Gt) to have a very high variance, making the gradient updates less stable and the learning process.
2. The Credit Assignment Problem: In very long trajectory, it's harder for agent to identify which action was responsible for a good or bad outcome at the end of the episode. 
3. Update Frequency: Longer trajectories means fewer updates over the same amount of interaction steps. Thus, it can slow down the initial learning phase compared to algorithms that update at every step.

Q3: How is the sample complexity (how many steps it takes to solve the environment) of this algorithm related to the DQN from the last exercise?
-------------------

Answer:

The sample complexity of the REINFORCE algorithm is significantly higher than that of DQN.
DQN achieved high stable rewards within just ~10,000 frames. Meanwhile, REINFORCE required hundreds of episodes (tens of thousands of frames) to reach a similar peak, and it frequently forgot its policy and crashed back to low scores.
This happens because DQN is off-policy and reuses past data via an Experience Replay buffer, making it very sample efficient. In contrast, REINFORCE is on-policy and discards trajectory data immediately after a single update.
