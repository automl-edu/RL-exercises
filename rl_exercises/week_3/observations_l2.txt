When training both agents,this were the findings:

| Metric               | SARSA          | Q-Learning     |
|----------------------|----------------|----------------|
| Final Reward         | 90.0           | 50.0           |
| Convergence Speed    | Faster         | Slower         |
| Stability            | More stable    | More volatile  |
| Exploration Impact   | High           | Lower          |

Learning behaviour comparison:

SARSA:
    - Starts with reward=9 (initial exploration)
    - Quickly jumps to 90 (optimal policy found early)
    - Occasionally drops to 0 or 50 (exploration phases)
    - Recovers quickly to 90

Q-Learning:
    - Also starts with reward=9
    - Reaches 90 but less consistently
    - More frequent drops to 0, 5, or 50

Difference in how they approach the problem:

    1. On-Policy vs Off-Policy:
        - SARSA: Learns current policy (with exploration)
        - Q-Learning: Learns optimal policy (ignores exploration)

    2. Update Rule:
        - SARSA: Conservative (uses actual next action)
        - Q-Learning: Optimistic (uses max Q-value)

    3. Exploration Impact:
        - SARSA: Handles exploration better in this case
        - Q-Learning: Suffers from overestimation (probably)
